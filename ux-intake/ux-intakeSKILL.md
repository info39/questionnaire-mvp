\# UX Intake Skill



\## Purpose

Generate a high-quality UX discovery questionnaire for client projects.



The questionnaire must deeply understand:

\- the business model

\- target audience

\- competitors

\- conversion goals

\- technical requirements



\## Thinking Approach



Before writing questions, always:

1\. Identify the type of project (landing page, ecommerce, website, system, etc.)

2\. Understand the business model (services, products, funnels)

3\. Consider the level of UX complexity required

4\. Identify potential conversion points

5\. Consider technical constraints (platform, integrations, automation)



\## Questionnaire Structure



The questionnaire must be divided into clear sections:



\### 1. Business Understanding

\- What does the business do?

\- What is the main value proposition?

\- What problem does it solve?

\- What makes it different from competitors?



\### 2. Target Audience

\- Who is the ideal customer?

\- What are their pain points?

\- What objections might they have?

\- What motivates them to take action?



\### 3. Goals \& Conversions

\- What is the main goal of the project?

\- What actions should users take?

\- What defines success?



\### 4. Competitors \& Inspiration

\- Who are the main competitors?

\- What do you like/dislike in their websites?

\- Are there reference websites?



\### 5. Content \& Messaging

\- Do you have existing content?

\- What tone should be used?

\- Are there key messages that must appear?



\### 6. Technical Requirements

\- Platform (WordPress, Shopify, custom)

\- Integrations (CRM, payment, automation)

\- Dynamic content needs

\- Performance considerations



\### 7. Design Direction

\- Brand guidelines

\- Preferred style

\- Visual references



\## Output Rules



\- The questionnaire must be clear, structured, and professional

\- Questions must be written in Hebrew

\- Tone: precise, confident, not generic

\- Avoid basic or obvious questions

\- Focus on questions that actually impact UX decisions



\## Format



\- Use headings for each section

\- Use bullet points for questions

\- Keep it client-friendly but professional
