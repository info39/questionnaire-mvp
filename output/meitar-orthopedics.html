Common Questions

We Answer Before You Have to Ask

Transparency is part of our care. Here are the questions every patient asks first.

Can't find your answer?

Does my Kupat Holim (HMO) cover treatment here?
▾
Yes — we work with all four major HMO funds: Maccabi, Clalit, Meuhedet, and Leumit. Most consultations and many procedures are fully covered under your standard kupat holim benefits. Our billing team will clarify your exact coverage before any treatment is agreed upon.
How quickly can I get an appointment?
▾
For routine consultations, we typically have availability within 3–5 business days. For urgent cases (acute injury, significant functional limitation), we prioritize same-day or next-day appointments. Call or WhatsApp us and we will find you a slot as quickly as possible.
Do I need a referral from my family doctor?
▾
A referral may be required depending on your HMO and the type of treatment. However, you are welcome to book a self-pay consultation at any time without a referral. Our administrative team will guide you through what's needed based on your specific fund and coverage.
What should I bring to my first appointment?
▾
Please bring your Israeli ID (teudat zehut), HMO membership card, any existing imaging (X-rays, MRI, CT scans), previous specialist reports, and a list of current medications. If you have prior surgical records, those are very helpful. Don't worry if you don't have everything — we can work with what you have.
What is your approach to surgery versus conservative treatment?
▾
Our philosophy is always conservative-first. Across all specialties, 94% of our patients achieve their outcomes without surgery through physical therapy, injection therapy, medication management, and guided lifestyle changes. Surgery is recommended only when it is clearly the best option — and we will always explain why in plain terms before any decision is made.
Is parking available at the clinic?
▾
Yes, the clinic has a private parking lot with 40 spaces, free for patients during appointments. We are also served by the Tel Aviv light rail (Ibn Gavirol stop, 3-minute walk) and multiple bus lines. Accessibility parking and wheelchair access are fully available at all entrances.
